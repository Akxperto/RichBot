GRADING POLICY, POSTGRADUATE
1. Overall final grades for courses are assigned by instructors at the end of every semester
or summer session. All grades are subject to confirmation at the Formal School Exam
Boards and University Exam Board.
2. Each letter grade is assigned a numerical equivalent on the following scale for
postgraduate study:
POSTGRADUATE GRADING SCALE
Grade Grade Point Descriptor
A 4.0 Excellent
A- 3.7 Very Good
B+ 3.3 Above satisfactory
B 3.0 Satisfactory (also cumulative GPA required for
award of the degree)
B- 2.7 Redeemable fail
C+ 2.3 Fail – Poor (may be awarded at graded activity
level, but not at course level)
C 2.0 Fail – Deficient (may be awarded at graded activity
level, but not at course level)
C- 1.7 Fail – Seriously Deficient (may be awarded at
graded activity level, but not at course level)
F 0.0 Fail (may be awarded at graded activity level, and
is awarded at course level for any course grade
calculated to be lower than B-)
FA 0.0 Fail (Attendance)
Additional grades that do not have a numerical equivalent and are not used in GPA
calculations are:
Grade Brief Descriptor
AU Audit
I Incomplete
P Pass (non-credit only)
TR Transfer Credit
TRP Transfer Credit (provisional)
W Withdrawn
