STUDENT VISA SPONSORSHIP UNDER THE STUDENT ROUTE POINTS
BASED SYSTEM
1. Students studying at partner institutions should contact Student Affairs for confirmation
of their immigration requirements and procedures.
2. Applicants requiring a Student Route Visa to study in the UK and under the University’s
Student Route Sponsor License are subject to the United Kingdom Visas & Immigration
regulations and guidance.
3. Applicants are notified of their Student Route status (or other relevant immigration
status) at the point of offer, and are issued with communications guiding them through
the Student Route Visa requirements and the process of obtaining a Confirmation for
Acceptance for Studies (CAS). Information about Student Route Visa requirements and
reference to the relevant UKVI information resources are made available to prospective
students at: http://www.richmond.ac.uk/immigration/.
4. Applicants who require a Student Visa to study in the UK will be issued with a
Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) once they have confirmed acceptance of
their Unconditional Study Offer and evidence the following:
a. advance payment of 40% of the annual tuition fee;
b. a valid passport (photo page);
c. academic qualifications;
d. valid and verified English as a Foreign Language Test result (where applicable);
e. a satisfactory pre-CAS credibility interview has taken place (where applicable);
f. evidence that the applicant meets the UKVI minimum thresholds for financial
maintenance which are:
i. the appropriate funds to cover the mandatory UKVI maintenance amount
for the duration of study;
ii. additional funds for any unpaid tuition;
iii. confirmation that funds of any unpaid fees (tuition) and UKVI
maintenance amount have been in current/deposit account for a period
of 28 days; or confirmation that the applicant is in receipt of funding from
source recognised by the UKVI (US Financial Aid, Educational Loan,
Sponsorship by International body)
iv. that funds are presented in the financial documentation format by the
UKVI.
5. The University will only issue a CAS if it is satisfied that it has, to the best of its ability,
verified the information provided by the applicant, to mitigate the risk of a visa refusal.
While the University makes every effort to minimize the risk of a visa refusal, it cannot
guarantee that an applicant’s visa application will be successful.
1

6. Applicants whose visa application has been refused are eligible for a full refund when
evidence of the visa application refusal is provided.
7. Applicants are required to present original evidence of their visa (30-day
vignette/electronic immigration status) and their original qualification prior to
enrolment.
8. Enrolled applicants who have been issued a Biometric Resident Permit (BRP) are
required to provide a copy to the University within 30 days of enrolment.
