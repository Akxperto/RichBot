MINORS AND US CERTIFICATES POLICY
Minor
1. This policy does not apply to UK-only top-up degrees.
2. A minor is an optional subject of specialisation within the undergraduate degree, outside
of the undergraduate major course of study, consisting of 18 US /72 UK credits (normally
6 courses). A minor may not be completed independent of an undergraduate degree.
3. Undergraduates who add a minor to their major degree programme will normally need
to complete more than the minimum 120 US/480 UK credits required for graduation.
4. At least three of the upper-division courses required for a minor must be completed at
the University.
5. No more than three courses may overlap within a degree between a student’s major
and any minor.
6. Upon graduation, any minor successfully completed is recorded on the student
transcript alongside the major.
US Certificate
7. The US certificate records the satisfactory completion of an organised programme at the
postsecondary level (below the AA degree). A US certificate consists of 15 US/60 UK
credits, normally 5 courses.
8. The US certificate is distinct from the UK CertHE as defined by the QAA.
9. The US Certificate is normally for study abroad or visiting students only, except for US
Certificates offered in one of the Italy locations which are also available to degree
seeking students.
10. Students must declare their certificate by submitting a completed form to Registry
Services.
11. Any US certificate successfully completed is recorded on the student transcript. For
study abroad and visiting students, this is done after their study at the University is
concluded. For degree-undergraduates who studied in Italy, this is done upon
graduation.
