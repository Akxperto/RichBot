UNDERGRADUATE DEGREE PROGRAMME STRUCTURE POLIC
1. The University awards both US and UK Bachelor’s degrees, the requirements of which
have been aligned by the University, so that the majority of undergraduates will receive
both degrees upon graduation. Students who qualify for the US Bachelor’s degree will
normally automatically qualify for the simultaneous award of the UK Bachelor’s degree.
2. A US/UK undergraduate degree consists of a minimum of 120 US / 480 UK credits. The
four-year US Bachelor’s degree contains one year of required credit at UK RQF Level 3. A
minimum of 30 US/ 120 UK credits is normally required at each FHEQ/RQF level,
although some majors may require slightly more depending on the nature of the degree.
3. Students admitted with advanced qualifications will be awarded transfer credit at RQF
Level 3, but additional L3 requirements may need to be completed. See Transfer Credit
Policy: Undergraduate.
4. The following definitions apply to degree-seeking undergraduates:
a. A freshman has earned fewer than 30 US/120 UK credits
b. A sophomore has earned between 30 US/120 UK credits and 59 US/236 UK
credits
c. A junior has earned between 60 US/240 UK credits and 89 US/356 UK credits
d. A senior has earned 90 US/360 UK or more credits.

6. The use of credit from a higher level to compensate for missing credit at a lower level
(“Dipdowns”) are permitted on a limited basis at the University subject to the Credit
Level Exception Policy.
7. Undergraduate students are subject to the degree requirements outlined in the
Programme Specification in place at the time of their admission to the University.
8. The University’s liberal arts undergraduate degrees (Bachelor of Arts or Bachelor of
Science) are composed of the following elements:
a. A Major course of study: an undergraduate’s “major” is the subject of
specialisation, indicated by the title of the degree, and is constructed within the
credit requirements;
b. Liberal Arts Core requirements: ten courses (30 US / 120 UK credits) intended to
provide a broad base of knowledge contextualizing the student’s major and to
develop mathematical, oral, written, and visual communication skills.
c. Electives: courses outside major requirements that complete credit level
requirements. These may contribute to a minor, or simply provide further
contextual study for the major.
9. Undergraduates are not permitted to enroll in postgraduate (FHEQ Level 7) courses.
