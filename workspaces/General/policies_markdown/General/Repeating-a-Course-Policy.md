REPEATING A COURSE POLICY
1. A student may repeat a course for which a grade of F or FA has been awarded.
2. The grade of F or FA remains on the student’s official academic transcript even if the
course is subsequently repeated and passed.
3. When a course is repeated and passed, the passing grade will be recorded on the
transcript and only this grade (not the original F/FA) will be used in computing the
cumulative GPA.
4. If a student withdraws from a repeated course, the W grade assigned will not replace
the original failing grade. The F grade will continue to be calculated in the student’s
GPA.
5. An F grade cannot be replaced by transfer credit.
6. A student may repeat a course for which a passing grade has been earned only with the
permission of the Academic Progress Committee. This may occur only once, and the
second grade earned is used for the GPA regardless whether the second grade awarded
is higher or lower.
7. Repeating a course may have US financial aid implications, and students petitioning to
repeat a course who are receiving financial aid should seek advice from the Finance
Office.
8. A course may not be repeated more than six times.
