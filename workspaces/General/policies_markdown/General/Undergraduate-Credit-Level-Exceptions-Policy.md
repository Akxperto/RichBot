UNDERGRADUATE CREDIT LEVEL EXCEPTIONS POLICY
1. A US/UK undergraduate degree consists of a minimum of 120 US / 480 UK credits. The
four-year US Bachelor’s degree contains one year of required credit at UK RQF Level 3. A
minimum of 30 US/ 120 UK credits is normally required at each FHEQ/RQF level,
although some majors may require slightly more depending on the nature of the degree.
2. On a case-by-case basis, the University approves limited amounts of additional credit
from Levels 6 and 5 to be counted down to satisfy missing elective credits at lower FHEQ
levels.
3. Credit Level count down approval is subject to:
a. All mandatory major requirements being fulfilled (including 30 US / 120 UK
credits at Level 6);
b. All Liberal Arts Core courses being fulfilled.
c. All learning outcomes being met.
4. A maximum of 6 US/24 UK credits (2 courses or an internship) may be counted down
from Level 6 to either Level 5 or Level 4.
5. The grades of additional Level 6 courses counted down to Level 5 will be used in the
Level 5 weighting of the UK GPA for the UK Degree Classification.
6. A maximum of 6 US/24 UK credits (2 courses) may be counted down from Level 5 to
Level 4.
7. Although a total of 12 additional credits may be used by an individual student to satisfy
missing credit at Levels 4 and 5 as outlined above, they must consist of a maximum of 6
credits at Level 6 and a maximum of 6 credits at Level 5.
8. More than 6 additional credits accumulated at Level 5 or Level 6 will be surplus to
student requirements in terms of credit level regulations (note that students pursuing a
minor/minors, engaging in study abroad, or who have changed majors etc. may find
themselves in the position of having additional credit at these levels).
9. There is no maximum on the amount of credits from Levels 6, 5 and 4 which may be
counted down to UK RQF Level 3 as electives.
10.Counting down of credit from a higher level to a lower one can be used to substitute for
major options only, not required major courses, subject to the normal approval
processes by the Associate Dean of the School, or Head of Department.
11.Counting down of credit from a higher level to a lower one can be used to substitute
within the Liberal Arts Core, subject to the normal approval processes by the Dean of
the School of Liberal Arts.
