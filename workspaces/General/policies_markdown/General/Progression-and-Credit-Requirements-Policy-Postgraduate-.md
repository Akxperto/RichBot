PROGRESSION AND CREDIT REQUIREMENTS POLICY:
POSTGRADUATE
Degree Programme Requirements
1. Postgraduate students are normally subject to the degree requirements outlined in the
Programme Specification in place at the time of their admission to the University. Re-
admitted students are normally subject to the degree requirements outlined in the
Programme Specification in place at the time of their re-admission to the University (see
Readmission Policy: Postgraduate).
Credit Requirements
2. MBA students must complete an approved programme of 36 US/180 UK credits. This
includes:
a. 12 credits of taught classes in the Fall and the Spring semesters (full time)
AND
b. a 4 US/20 UK credit internship and an 8 US/40 UK research project of 10,000
words(MBA)
OR
c. 12 US/60 UK research project of 15,000 words
3. MA students must complete an approved programme of 36 US/180 UK credits. This
includes:
a. 12 credits of taught classes in the Fall and the Spring semesters (full time)
AND
b. a 4 US/20 UK credit internship and an 8 US/40 UK credit thesis/professional
research project of 10,000 words
OR
c. a 12 US/60 UK credit thesis/professional research project of 15,000 words.
4. A maximum of 12 US/ 60 UK masters-level transfer credits may be awarded upon entry.
See Transfer Credit Policy: Postgraduate.
Coursework Requirements and Late Submission of Coursework
5. Postgraduate students must successfully complete all coursework, and maintain good
academic standing.
6. All students on Masters programmes are required to be in London from early May to
mid-June for thesis/professional research project supervision and seminars unless their
internship takes them outside London.
7. Postgraduates must submit all coursework on time. A student may request an extension
to a coursework submission deadline from the instructor before the published deadline
has passed.

8. Postgraduates without approved extensions and submit coursework late incur grade
deductions according to the policy relating to the Late Submission of Coursework Policy.
9. Where students have mitigating circumstances for the late submission, the instructor
should be informed in advance by email, and evidence of good cause should be provided
to the instructor in writing when the coursework is submitted.
Thesis/Dissertation and Internship Requirements
10.Students who do not submit the thesis/dissertation will receive an F grade for the
thesis/dissertation component.
11.Students with mitigating circumstances supported by official documentation may
petition the Academic Progress Committee, for an extension to the deadline for
submission of their thesis/professional research project. Please see Incompletes,
Rescheduled and Delayed Submission of Coursework: Postgraduate Policy.
12.Where an extension to the submission deadline is approved, the due date will be no
later than Week 4 of the following semester, or an earlier date will be determined on a
case-by-case basis by the Associate Dean in consultation with the Academic Registrar
and the Academic Progress Committee.
13.In cases of thesis/dissertation resubmission, the thesis/dissertation will be considered at
the Assessment Board of the following academic year. The diploma, if granted, will
therefore be dated one academic year later than if the thesis/dissertation had been
submitted on time.
