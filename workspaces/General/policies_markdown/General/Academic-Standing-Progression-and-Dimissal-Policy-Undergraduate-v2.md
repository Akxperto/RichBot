ACADEMIC STANDING, PROGRESSION AND DISMISSAL POLICY:
UNDERGRADUATE
For students registered on the University’s US and UK awards:
1. Good academic standing for undergraduate students is defined as a cumulative GPA of
at least 2.000 (C) in at least 18 US/72 UK credits earned over the two most recent
consecutive semesters.
2. At the end of any full semester (fall or spring), fulltime degree-seeking undergraduates
and visiting students for more than one semester who have attained a cumulative grade
point average (GPA) of less than 2.000 will be identified by the Academic Progress
Committee and be advised that they have been placed on academic probation for the
next semester.
3. While on academic probation undergraduates are limited to enroll in a maximum of
four courses per fall or spring semester. This limit is designed to allow more time to
concentrate on each course and improve academic performance.
4. Courses taken in the summer sessions will not result in the lifting of probationary
status. Undergraduates on academic probation achieving a cumulative GPA of 2.000 or
above in a summer session will remain on academic probation in the following semester
until they have demonstrated sustained improvement and progression over a full 15-
week semester.
5. Failure to raise the GPA to 2.000 by the end of a second full semester will normally
result in dismissal from the University for failure to progress academically.
6. Undergraduates admitted to the University on academic probation are treated as if
their first semester of attendance is their first semester on academic probation.
7. Full-time undergraduates who do not earn 18 US credits over the two most recent
semesters (including a semester of no study when not approved for a Leave of Absence)
are at risk of academic dismissal.
8. Dismissal decisions are made each full semester, at the University Examination Board,
upon the recommendation of the Academic Progress Committee.
9. Students who are academically dismissed from the University have the right to appeal
the decision to the Provost within five working days of receiving the decision via email
10. Students in the UK on a Tier 4 visa risk withdrawal of their right to remain if they fail to
make acceptable academic progress. The University is obliged to report any student
who is dismissed from the University and who is in the UK on a student visa, to UK Visas

For students registered on the University’s UK-only awards:
12. Good academic standing for undergraduate students is defined as a cumulative GPA of
at least 2.000 (C) in at least 80 UK credits earned over the two most recent consecutive
semesters.

and visiting students for more than one semester who have attained a cumulative grade

Committee and be advised that they have been placed on academic probation for the
next semester.
14. While on academic probation undergraduates are limited to enroll in a maximum of two
courses per fall or spring semester. This limit is designed to allow more time to
concentrate on each course and improve academic performance.
15. Courses taken in the summer sessions will not result in the lifting of probationary
status. Undergraduates on academic probation achieving a cumulative GPA of 2.000 or
above in a summer session will remain on academic probation in the following semester
until they have demonstrated sustained improvement and progression over a full 15-
week semester.
16. Failure to raise the GPA to 2.000 by the end of a second full semester will normally
result in dismissal from the University for failure to progress academically.
17. Undergraduates admitted to the University on academic probation are treated as if
their first semester of attendance is their first semester on academic probation.
18. Full-time undergraduates who do not earn 80 UK credits over the two most recent
semesters (including a semester of no study when not approved for a Leave of Absence)
are at risk of academic dismissal.
19. After two semesters or more on APP, students who have been unable to earn 80 UK
credits over the two most recent consecutive semesters, risk dismissal from the
University.
20. Dismissal decisions are made each full semester, at the University Examination Board,
upon the recommendation of the Academic Progress Committee.
