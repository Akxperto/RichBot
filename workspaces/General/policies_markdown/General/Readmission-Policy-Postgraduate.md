READMISSION POLICY: POSTGRADUATE
1. Postgraduates who have officially or unofficially withdrawn from the University, or who
have been away from the University past the expiry date of an approved leave of
absence, must apply to Registry Services for re-admission using the form on the
university website.
2. Applications for re-entry to either full-time or part-time study must be submitted at
least twelve weeks before the semester begins, and additional material may be
requested to inform the University’s decision to re-admit.
3. Re-admission is only considered within four years of the last semester in which students
were registered for courses at the University.
4. Postgraduates dismissed from the University on academic grounds are not normally
granted readmission.
5. Postgraduates are normally required to be in good academic standing to be considered
for re-admission.
6. Applications for re-admission are not accepted from students who have already
graduated from the University with a UK and/or a US postgraduate Exit Award, unless
they have not attempted the dissertation. See Exit Awards Policy - Postgraduate.
7. Confirmation deposits are required for re-admission.
8. Re-admitted students are registered onto the programme requirements in place during
their semester of re-admission, not the requirements in place during their last semester
of study. Course cycling may mean that postgraduates will be restricted as to in which
semester they may readmit, and so may encounter delays to progression.
9. Re-admitted students may register for classes with the assistance of Registry Services at
any time after the approved re-admission and the necessary financial and immigration
steps have been completed.
