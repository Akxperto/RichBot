ACADEMIC STANDING, PROGRESSION AND DISMISSAL POLICY:
POSTGRADUATE
1. Postgraduate students are aware of the level of their academic progress throughout
their study and those performing below B (3.0) in any course will normally be alerted
to this at mid-semester by the Programme Director/Convenor.
2. Postgraduates with an overall GPA of less than 3.0 (B) at the end of the first
semester will be formally written to by the Associate Dean of the School to explain
the consequences of a GPA below good academic standing for a second semester.
3. The academic records of all postgraduate students are reviewed at the end of the
second semester. Two consecutive semesters with an overall GPA of less than 3.0 (B)
will normally result in dismissal from the University.
4. Any student permitted to continue studies to re-take courses will not normally be
permitted to progress to the Thesis or Internship until their GPA is 3.0 or above.
