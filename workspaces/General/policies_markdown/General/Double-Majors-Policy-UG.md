DOUBLE MAJORS POLICY: UNDERGRADUATE
1. This policy does not apply to UK-only top-up degrees.
2. An undergraduate may complete the requirements of two majors and receive one US
degree with those two majors. The student’s official academic transcript will indicate
one US degree conferred with two majors, and the student will receive one diploma.
3. Only one of the majors can be awarded for the UK degree and recorded on the UK
degree certificate. Students must nominate their chosen UK degree on their application
for graduation.
4. Where students are completing a double major, at least 15 US/60 UK Level 6 credits
must be unique to each major and cannot have been used in any minor or in the liberal
arts core. These credits must include Senior Seminar 1 and 2 for each major.
5. For double majors completing minors, three of the courses on the minor must not
overlap with either of the major requirements.
6. Students completing two majors will need to complete more than 120 US/480 UK
credits.
7. Students may not be registered for more than two majors, and cannot be awarded for a
degree recognising more than two majors.
