CREDIT HOUR POLICY: POSTGRADUATE
1. At the postgraduate level, one US credit is equivalent to:
a. 2.5 European Credit Transfer System (ECTS) credits
b. 5 UK credits
2. The credit equivalency for US and UK credits at the Masters level differs from that at the
undergraduate level, as additional independent study hours are required at the Masters
level. This is reflected in the notional hours of learning calculated for credit in the UK,
but the US credit calculation remains rooted in classroom hours.
3. A typical postgraduate course consists of 4 US/20 UK credits (for US/UK credit
equivalencies, see below). 4 US postgraduate credits / 20 UK postgraduate credits
normally correspond to classroom hours as follows:
a. 80 minute classes that meet 2 times per week (terminating 10 minutes before
the 90-minute timetabling end point)
b. 160 minute classes that meet 3 times per week (containing a break of 10 minutes
and terminating 10 minutes before the 180-minute timetabling end point).
4. The difference between undergraduate and post-graduate ratios of classroom time to
credit is a result of increased expectations for research time outside of the classroom.
5. For out of class room experiences, such as internships, one ‘practice’ credit hour
represents 3-4 hours per week of supervised and/or independent practice. This in turn
represents between 45 and 60 hours of work per semester per credit. The hours
expressed in the Course Specification Documents (CSDs) should fall within this range.
