REFUNDS AND WITHDRAWALS POLICY
1. Situations may arise where a student chooses to withdraw from the University. These policies
and procedures provide the financial regulations governing this.
2. New students who require a visa to study in the UK are not normally eligible for a refund of
deposits or fees. However if a visa denial letter from the UKVI is issued and forwarded to the
Finance Department within four weeks, deposits and fees will be refunded.
3. For existing students to be eligible for a tuition refund, the student must have completed fully
and within the necessary time frame the University’s requirements for payment and withdrawal.
To withdraw, a student must complete the University’s withdrawal form (see University
Withdrawal Policy). Any student in receipt of U.S Federal Loans must complete the U.S
government’s Exit Counselling. Information on this can be provided at the University’s Office of
Financial Aid.
4. Where refunds are possible, they will be made only when the student has followed and
correctly completed all of the University’s rules and procedures prior to the request of a refund.
This includes the payment of the application fee. No refunds of deposits or fees will be made if a
student is dismissed from the University on the grounds of academic misconduct, breach of the
Student Code of Conduct, or failure to make academic progress.
5. Where a student believes that they are due a refund in relation to this policy, they must formally
request to do so. The refund request form can be obtained from refunds@richmond.ac.uk
Confirmation Deposits (USD students only)
6. US confirmation deposits are not refundable or transferable when a student does not enrol at
the University. If a US student enrols, the confirmation deposit may be offset against their
tuition fees.
Housing Refunds
7. Once housing is applied for and granted, the fee payer is liable for the full amount of the
housing, even if the student withdraws or takes an approved LoA or is dismissed from housing
for disciplinary reasons. Housing deposits and fees will be refunded if a student is denied a visa
to enter the United Kingdom. Housing deposit refunds can be sought through the normal refund
process.
Tuition Refunds: Existing Students and New Students Without a Visa

LoA appropriately and in the time frames specified. The amount of refund depends on when the
withdrawal process is completed:

9. Students on a payment plan should note that the percentage refund is the percentage of the full
tuition price. Students changing from full time to part time status are liable to the refund levels
and timetable as detailed above.
10. Where a student has paid for a full year of tuition the above percentages are applied to each
semester individually. For example, if an undergraduate student were to officially withdraw the
week after the First Week of Classes in Fall, they would receive 0% for their Fall semester but a
100% semester refund for their Spring semester. This applies to new students with a visa too,
but doesn’t apply to RIASA students that commit to an entire academic year (see RIASA Fees
Policy).
Special Fee Conditions for Postgraduates
11.The following terms and conditions are specific to postgraduate students, otherwise all terms
and conditions between postgraduates and undergraduates are identical.
12.Fees are payable in UK Pounds Sterling.
13.Payments are due in three equal instalments:01 August (Fall semester), 01 December (Spring
semester), 01 May (Summer semester).
14.A 100% refund will be made for tuition fees less any confirmation deposit if an official
withdrawal is made before 5pm on the Friday before the first week of classes in the Fall or
Spring semesters. Thereafter, no refunds are made.
15.However, if the University has issued a Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) and a
student visa has been issued there will be no refunds of tuition.
